What is Touch?

History

  • Global community of over 40 countries
  • 6 previous World Cups held (numerous other international tournaments)

Benefits of Touch

  • Minimal contact
  • Suitable for all ages & genders
  • Maximum player involvement
  • Team sport attributes

How Touch Differs

Touch Rugby
Minimal contact Full contact
6 players on the field 7 - 15 players on the field (depending on format)
Continuous (rolling) substitutions Managed substitutions
6 touches > change of possession Unlimited possesion (Rugby Union)
No kicking Kicking allowed

Playing Area

Basic Rules

  • Attacking team has 6 "touches" to move the ball downfield and score.
  • After a touch is made:
    • The attacker performs a rollball at the mark they are touched.
    • Defending team must retreat 7m after each touch is made.
  • A try is scored when a player has possession of the ball & grounds it in the in-goal area.
  • Change of possession occurs:
    • After 6 touches
    • If control of the ball is lost by the attacking team
    • If the attacking team commits a penalty offence
  • Substitutions can happen at any time through the interchange area.
